## Traffic Modelling Assumptions

All projections are based on the following modelling parameters:

- **Peak-to-average ratio**: 3× (peak concurrent streams at prime time = 3× daily average)
- **Video average bitrate at peak**: 4 Mbps (HLS adaptive bitrate; mix of 720p and 1080p dominant quality tier)
- **Audio average bitrate at peak**: 192 kbps (stereo AAC-LC)
- **Nigeria content fraction**: 10% of total catalogue; 15% of total playback sessions
- **Nigeria PoP offload rate**: 70% (30% cache miss served from af-south-1 origin)
- **Global CDN offload rate**: 85% (15% cache miss rate for long-tail content)

## Nigeria-Specific Traffic

| Metric | Year 1 | Year 3 |
|---|---|---|
| Nigeria DAU | 45,000 (30% of total) | 200,000 (27% of total) |
| Peak concurrent streams (Nigeria) | 50,000 | 150,000 |
| Percentage served from MTN PoP | 70% | 75% |
| Nigeria peak egress (MTN PoP) | ~140 Gbps | ~420 Gbps |
| Nigeria peak egress (af-south-1 origin) | ~42 Gbps | ~105 Gbps |

<Note>
  Nigeria traffic projections assume 70% CDN PoP offload at Year 1. Increasing the MTN PoP cache capacity by Year 3 (target 75% offload) requires capacity planning with MTN networks. Origin egress from `af-south-1` is significantly more expensive per GB than CDN delivery, making PoP offload rate a cost-critical metric.
</Note>
